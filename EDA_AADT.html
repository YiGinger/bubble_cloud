Purpose

To get a sense of what the traffic is likein New Yorkover 1977-2011so to provide insight about the traffic direct market. In this exploratory analysis report, we mainly investigated the vehicle numbers of each recorded trip.

Data Source

Annual Average TrafficData (AADT) https://data.ny.gov/Transportation/Annual-Average-Daily-Traffic-AADT-Beginning-1977/6amx-2pbv

Data

The data originally comes with 11variables.One variable was created.

Original Variables

Some text variables used to describe the item: RC_ID; Roadway Begin Description; Roadway End Description;

Other original variables:

  • Name: Name of the roadway the AADT was calculated for
  • Roadway Type: 3 types of roadway for which the AADT was collected, including route, road and ramp, along with an unspecified one (with 133 observations).
  • GIS Code: Unique ID assigned to the roadway for which the AADT was collected
  • Begin of MilePoint: The starting milepoint of the segment of roadway for which the AADT applies
  • End of MilePoint: The ending milepoint of the segment of roadway for which the AADT appliesMunicipality: 1472 municipalities
  • Year: year 1977to year 2011
  • Vehicles: Annual Average Daily Traffic (AADT) in Vehicles ### Created variable
  • Mile Point: the difference between the Begin of Mile point and the End of Mile Point.

Data Manipulation

There were 113 observations with missing fields in Roadway Type, Begin of Mile Point, End of Mile Point, and GIS Code; when Mile Pointwas calculated, the missing entries are carried forward.In the exploration of Mile Point, the 113 observations were removed.

Exploratory Analysis

We have to be mindful that the observations increased over the year so sum partially reflects that, while mean does not.We also have to remember that the observations in route roadway type is greater than road and road is greater than ramp; sum also will be affected by this fact.

AADT in Vehicles by Year

Exploratory Analysis in Distribution

We might want to remove the two extremes in 1987;however, comparing to the sample size, two are really negligible. As to the rest of the outliers, since they followsimilar patterns, they should not have been human entry error. They are leftin the data for now so we can see the big picture.

There is a slightlyincreasing trend in the median of traffic before the 90s and then decreasing trend after that. As we can also see that the density of the number of vehicles larger than the median is lower than that of smaller than the median value.

Average Trend

The items in the dataset is not all the road in the US and the number of items in each year is different, so it makes no sense to compare the sum of the number of Vehiles in this dataset for each year , and we can look into median and mean of number of Vehicles and see the trend.

If we take the average, the mean of vehicles actually increased to the maximum around year 1996 and then decreased again. Except for great changes from 1998 to 2000, the variation is smooth. However, the analysis of average is not always enough. In order to understand the data in more aspect, we may consider more variables and obtain more detailed information of the number of vehicles.

Consideration in Roadway type

The graph above is the number of vehicles for each type of roadway. We can see that the changes in these three types of roadway are very different from the average, so they can have different impact on the average number of vehicles. The number of Ramp varies with high variance through time. In comparison, the number of Route in each year differs slightly.

As you can see that the observations in route roadway type is greater than road and road is greater than ramp. It means the weight of ramp in the influence to the average vehicles for the whole year is relatively small. Althought the number changes so much, the stability in the number of route make the average number of vehicles relatively smooth. And this can also explain why the average number of vehicles changes sharply between 1998 to 2000. According to the data, there is no ramp included in the dataset in 1998. From 1999 to 2000, the average number of ramps declined sharply from 16720 to 2050. The great changes in ramp leads the mean of the number of vehicles to be very unstable during these three years.
